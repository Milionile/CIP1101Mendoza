"LUNETA PARK"

"Liwasang Rizal/Bagumbayan"


Also known as Luneta Park or simply Luneta, is a historic urban park located in Ermita, Manila. It is considered one of the largest urban parks in the Philippines, covering an area of 58 hectares (140 acres). The site on where the park is situated was originally known as Bagumbayan during the Spanish colonial period. It is adjacent to the historic Walled City of Intramuros.


The park's history dates back to 1820 when it was known as Bagumbayan. It became significant during the Spanish colonial period as the site of Dr. Jos√© Rizal's execution on December 30, 1896, which fueled the Philippine Revolution. The park was later named in his honor, with a monument enshrining his remains. During the American colonial period, it was the venue for the declaration of Philippine independence from the United States on July 4, 1946. Luneta Park has witnessed numerous historical events, including political rallies and the People Power Revolution of 1986. It is managed by the National Parks Development Committee and remains a symbol of Philippine history and nationalism, featuring the iconic Rizal Monument and various gardens and museums.
